Tarlac

Tarlac is a province in the Philippines situated in the Central Luzon region occupying the central section of Luzon. Its capital is the City of Tarlac. The province has a land area of 3,046.49 square kilometers or 1,176.26 square miles. Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 1,503,456. This represented 12.10% of the total population of the Central Luzon region, 2.42% of the overall population of the Luzon island group, or 1.38% of the entire population of the Philippines. Based on these figures, the population density is computed at 494 inhabitants per square kilometer or 1,278 inhabitants per square mile.

Belen Capital

Belen is a 3D art portraying the Birth of Christ. The Department of Tourism has given Tarlac the name "Belen Capital of the Philippines" for displaying skillfully designed Belens for the past years to show solidarity with Belenismo Festival in Tarlac.

Popular Spots

Monasterio de Tarlac

A symbolic statue of Tarlac siuated on top of a mountain in San Jose. You can see a great view of the vast green plains of Tarlac. Many farms and neighboring mountains can be seen.

Capas National Shrine

Capas National Shrine or Capas Shrine is where the many names of soldiers who fought in the World War are located. Their names are engraven on walls surrounding the large obelisk in the middle.

Tarlac Recreational Park

Tarlac Recreational Park, most people in Tarlac call by it's acronym "TRP". A good place for exercise and athletes to practice. A large oval, a stage, race track, ponds and pools can also be found in TRP.
